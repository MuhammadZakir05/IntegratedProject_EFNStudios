What to do if you've been scammed

  1. Stop all communication with the scammer
  2. Document everything (messages, transactions, dates)
  3. Report to authorities immediately
  4. Contact your bank or credit card company
  5. Change passwords if you shared any information
  6. Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity

How to protect yourself

  • Never share personal or financial information with unsolicited contacts
  • Verify identities through official channels
  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Keep software and antivirus updated
  • Be skeptical of urgent requests for money or information

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get my money back if I've been scammed?

A: It depends on the payment method. Contact your bank or credit card company immediately. Recovery rates are low, but acting fast improves your chances.

Q: Should I feel ashamed about being scammed?

A: Absolutely not. Scammers are professionals who exploit psychology. Anyone can be a victim. Reporting helps protect others.

Q: Is it safe to engage with scammers to waste their time?

A: Not recommended. Engaging can expose you to risks and scammers may retaliate. It's best to block and report immediately.
