Phishing Scams

Email and message-based fraud

Phishing scams trick victims into revealing sensitive information through fake emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources.

Common Signs:

  • Suspicious email addresses or URLs
  • Urgent language demanding immediate action
  • Requests for personal or financial information
  • Poor grammar and spelling errors

Investment Scams

Fake opportunities and ponzi schemes

Investment scams promise high returns with little or no risk. They often use social pressure and fake testimonials to lure victims.

Warning Signs:

  • Guaranteed or unusually high returns
  • Pressure to invest quickly
  • Unregistered investment opportunities
  • Complex strategies that are hard to understand

Employment Scams

Fake job offers and work-from-home schemes

Employment scams offer fake jobs that require upfront payments or steal personal information during the "application" process.

Red Flags:

  • Job offers without an application or interview
  • Requests for money for training or equipment
  • Vague job descriptions
  • Unrealistic salary for the work required

Romance Scams

Fake relationships for financial gain

Romance scammers create fake profiles on dating sites and social media to build relationships, then ask for money.

Warning Signs:

  • Professes love very quickly
  • Claims to be overseas or unable to meet in person
  • Asks for money for emergencies or travel
  • Avoids video calls or in-person meetings

Tech Support Scams

Fake technical assistance

Scammers pose as tech support from well-known companies, claiming your computer has a virus or security issue.

Common Tactics:

  • Unsolicited pop-ups claiming virus infection
  • Cold calls from "Microsoft" or "Apple"
  • Request remote access to your computer
  • Charge for unnecessary services or software

General Protection Tips

  • Always verify before you trust - check official websites and phone numbers
  • Never share personal or financial information through unsecured channels
  • Be skeptical of offers that seem too good to be true
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication
  • Keep your software and security systems updated
  • Report suspected scams to authorities immediately
